Overall Model Fit

The regression model tests whether pace, free throw percentage, rebounds, turnovers, and three-point percentage are related to team winning percentage.

A low overall model p-value means that the predictors, taken together, help explain variation in winning percentage better than a model with no predictors.

Adjusted R-squared measures how much of the variation in winning percentage is explained by the model after accounting for the number of predictors.

These two values measure different things. A model can be statistically significant even if Adjusted R-squared is not close to 1.

This is common in sports analytics because winning percentage depends on many other factors, including defense, injuries, coaching, player availability, schedule strength, and team chemistry.

Summary

The model suggests that rebounds, turnovers, and pace are important variables for explaining team winning percentage in this dataset.

Rebounds have a positive relationship with winning percentage.

Turnovers have a negative relationship with winning percentage.

Pace has a modest relationship with winning percentage.

Free throw percentage and three-point percentage may not be statistically significant after controlling for the other variables in this sample.

The model has moderate explanatory power overall, which is reasonable because basketball outcomes depend on many factors beyond these five statistics.
